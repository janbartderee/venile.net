Natural challanges in urban deltas

Urban deltas

Coastal cities and urban deltas, where land meets the sea, are located in dynamic and vital ecosystems. These intricate landscapes are home to over half a billion people worldwide, supporting fisheries, agriculture, cities, and diverse ecosystems. However, their existence is under threat from a convergence of natural challenges and human actions.
On this website we dive into the natural challanges of two coastal regions: the Venetian Lagoon (Italy) and the Nile River Delta (Egypt).

Natural challanges

Rising global temperatures lead to melting glaciers and ice sheets, contributing to a rise in sea levels. Coastal cities and deltas are particularly vulnerable to this phenomenon. As sea levels creep upward, these low-lying areas face inundation, erosion, and saltwater intrusion. Additionally, human activities, such as groundwater extraction and urban development, cause land subsidence, weakening the protective buffer against flooding. Rapid urbanization further strains delta regions, disrupting natural sediment delivery processes and affecting land elevation. Urgent action is needed to safeguard these vital regions, balancing human needs with ecological resilience.
